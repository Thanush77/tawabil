How to Identify Pure Spices

Green Cardamom

Elettaria cardamomum

Signs of Pure Cardamom

  • Bright green color with fresh appearance
  • Plump, heavy pods that don't feel hollow
  • Seeds inside are dark brown/black and sticky with oil
  • Strong, sweet aroma when crushed
  • Pods are closed and intact

Crush a pod between your fingers. Authentic cardamom releases a strong, sweet, eucalyptus-like aroma immediately.

Black Pepper

Piper nigrum

Signs of Pure Pepper

  • Uniform dark brown to black color
  • Wrinkled surface with visible texture
  • Heavy weight ‚Äì sinks quickly in water
  • Pungent, sharp aroma
  • Hot, biting taste when chewed

‚ö†Ô∏è Watch for: Lightweight peppercorns that float, uniform smooth surface (may be papaya seeds), or lack of pungency.

üî¨ Water Test

Drop peppercorns in water. Pure pepper sinks immediately. Adulterated or low-quality pepper floats or sinks slowly.

Ceylon Cinnamon

Cinnamomum verum

Signs of True Cinnamon

  • Light tan to medium brown color
  • Multiple thin, papery layers (like a cigar)
  • Easily breaks or crumbles when pressed
  • Sweet, delicate, complex aroma
  • Soft texture when ground

‚ö†Ô∏è Cassia vs Ceylon: Cassia (fake cinnamon) is thicker, harder, darker red-brown, and has a single curled layer. High coumarin content in cassia can be harmful.

üî¨ Break Test

True Ceylon cinnamon crumbles easily when pressed. Cassia is hard and splinters. Ceylon has multiple delicate layers visible.

Cloves

Syzygium aromaticum

Signs of Pure Cloves

  • Rich reddish-brown color
  • Intact bud shape with visible head
  • Oily texture ‚Äì releases oil when pressed with nail
  • Strong, warm, sweet-spicy aroma
  • Sinks vertically in water (head up)

‚ö†Ô∏è Watch for: Dry, brittle cloves with no oil, black color (over-dried), or cloves that float horizontally in water.

üî¨ Oil Test

Press a clove with your fingernail. Fresh, pure cloves release visible oil and leave an oily mark. Spent cloves are dry.

Cumin Seeds

Cuminum cyminum

Signs of Pure Cumin

  • Uniform brownish-yellow color
  • Elongated, ridged boat shape
  • Distinct earthy, warm aroma
  • Bitter-sweet taste when chewed
  • Clean, no visible dust or debris

‚ö†Ô∏è Watch for: Excess dust or powder (may be mixed with grass seeds), uniform dark color, or lack of distinctive cumin aroma.

üî¨ Rub Test

Rub cumin seeds between your palms. Pure cumin releases its distinctive warm, earthy aroma immediately. Adulterated cumin smells grassy or has no smell.

Turmeric

Curcuma longa

Signs of Pure Turmeric

  • Deep orange-yellow color (not bright yellow)
  • Earthy, musky, slightly bitter smell
  • Hard texture for whole turmeric
  • Slightly rough powder texture
  • Stains fingers a natural yellow-orange

‚ö†Ô∏è Watch for: Bright artificial yellow color, smooth silky texture (may have starch), or chemical smell indicating metanil yellow dye.

üî¨ Water Test

Add turmeric to water. Pure turmeric takes time to dissolve and leaves no sediment. Adulterated turmeric (with metanil yellow) leaves yellow water immediately with chalk-like sediment.

Preservation

How to Store Spices

Proper storage extends shelf life and preserves flavor.

üå°Ô∏è

Cool & Dark

Store away from heat sources and direct sunlight to prevent oil degradation.

ü´ô

Airtight Containers

Use glass or food-grade containers with tight seals to prevent moisture entry.

üíß

Keep Dry

Never use wet spoons. Moisture causes clumping and mold growth.

Check Expiry

Whole spices last 2-3 years. Ground spices are best used within 6-12 months.
