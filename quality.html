Our Process

Farm to Fork Journey

We ensure that our spices retain their natural oils, aroma, and health benefits through a meticulous sourcing process.

Step 1

Sourcing

We partner directly with farmers in Kerala, Rajasthan, and Tamil Nadu who practice sustainable agriculture. Our team visits farms personally to ensure ethical practices.

Direct farmer partnerships Sustainable practices Fair trade pricing
Step 2

Selection

Only the finest produce is selected based on size, color, aroma, and essential oil content. We reject over 40% of harvest that doesn't meet our standards.

Visual inspection Aroma testing Oil content analysis
Step 3

Processing

Spices are cleaned, graded, and processed in FSSAI-compliant hygienic facilities. We use cold processing methods to preserve natural oils and nutrients.

Hygienic facilities Cold processing Quality grading
Step 4

Packaging

Packed in food-grade, airtight containers with nitrogen flushing to lock in freshness. Our packaging extends shelf life while preserving aroma.

Airtight containers Nitrogen flushing UV protection

The Purity Test

How to Identify Pure Spices

We believe an educated customer is our best customer.

Adulteration is common in the spice market. Here's how you can tell Tawabil spices apart from the rest:

Pure Authentic Cardamom

Plump, green pods that feel heavy. When opened, seeds appear dark brown/black and are sticky with oil.

Pure Real Cinnamon

Tan brown color, thin multiple layers like a cigar, easily breakable, and has a sweet, delicate aroma.

Pure Black Pepper

Uniform dark brown/black color with a wrinkled surface. Should sink in water (heavy), not float (light/hollow).
